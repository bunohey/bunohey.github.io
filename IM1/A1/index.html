1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed was the loading animation, which made it feel like I was launching a new app.
Then, the large text "Welcome to the Luniverse" immediately gave me the feeling of entering a new world, creating a strong sense of belonging.

 

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

I spent two minutes interacting with various 3D objects, playing mini-games like soccer and basketball,
and experimenting with small interactive elements like a laptop, fried eggs, and a punching bag.

 

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I was impressed by hovering over objects and watching how the 3D elements popped up and rotated based on the mouse movement.
It was fun seeing how the perspective changed dynamically.

 

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The site had smooth, soft transitions that made the interactions feel seamless when scrolling and hovering over elements.

 

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I think the primary goal seems to be immersing users in a playful, interactive digital space while introducing Luni as a next-generation brand.

 

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Through engaging 3D interactions, fun mini-games, and an open-ended exploration style, it makes users feel like they are stepping into a unique digital universe.

 

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

It felt like an experience designed to be revisited multiple times, as there are many interactive elements and hidden details to discover.

 

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The smooth transitions, dynamic 3D effects, and game-like interactions suggest that users should explore the site at their own pace and return to experience more.

 

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website references the metaverse, digital games, and immersive web experiences, positioning itself as a futuristic and interactive brand platform.

 

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I was engaged with curiosity and playfulness, exploring the space freely rather than following a strict structure.

 

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

It received a sense of excitement, adventure, and curiosity, making users feel like they are part of something futuristic and innovative.

 

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Because the website felt like a 3D world, the longer I looked at the site, I felt more tired and slightly dizzy.

 

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The way the 3D objects reacted to the mouse movement made the whole experience feel immersive and futuristic.
Additionally, the low-saturation color palette combined with the cute 3D objects created a cozy and charming atmosphere.
